HTML Heading

The HTML heading tag is used to define the headings of a page. There are six levels of headings defined by HTML. These 6 heading elements are h1, h2, h3, h4, h5, and h6; with h1 being the highest level and h6 being the least.

1." h1 is used to the main heading.(biggest in size)

2. h2 is usde for subheadings, if there are further sections under the subheadings then the h3 elements are used.

3. h6 for the small heading (smallest one)

Syntax

// the 'h' inside the tag should be in small case only.

Heading1

Heading2

. . .
Heading6

Importance of Heading:

1. Search Engines use headings for indexing the structure and organizing the content of the webpage.

2. Headings are used for highlighting important topics.

3. They provide valuable information and tell us about the structure of the document.
